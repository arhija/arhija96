4 Portions - 40 Minutes

A quick and cheap hearty meal.

Ingredients:

  • 450g Udon noodles
  • One head of broccoli
  • Fresh chili peppers
  • One large white onion
  • Half a clove of garlic
  • Soy sauce
  • Honey
  • Dijon mustard

Preparation:

  1. Wash and cut the broccoli. Take a large pot with water and a bit of salt and wait for it to boil. Add the broccoli to it and cook until soft (3-4 minutes). While cutting the broccoli, there will be small particles left; add those to a frying pan.

  2. Cut the large white onion and add it to a frying pan with some oil of your choosing. Cut 3 chili peppers into the smallest pieces possible and add them to the frying pan. When the onions are close to being ready, chop a couple of cloves of garlic and add them to the pan.

  3. By this time, the broccoli will be ready. Pour the water out.

  4. For the sauce, you will need soy sauce, honey, and Dijon mustard. Add all of the ingredients together in a bowl and mix them. (60% soy sauce, 20% honey, 20% Dijon mustard.)

  5. Start making the noodles. You can use any kind you want, but I prefer to use dried Udon noodles. Use 4 portion sizes.

  6. Add the broccoli to the pan with the onions, chili, and garlic. Keep it on low heat and add the sauce. We want to thicken it.

  7. When the noodles are done cooking, add them to the pan, mix everything together, and cook for a couple of minutes until all of the ingredients are well combined.

  8. Garnish with chopped fresh spring onions.

  9. Enjoy it with friends.
